Positive for HPV

A positive result does not mean you have cervical cancer, however it does mean you are at risk of having changes to your cervix.

What this means

A positive test result means that high risk HPV (human papillomavirus) has been found on your sample.

This doesn't indicate you have cervical cancer, it does signify you are at risk of changes in your cervix.

If left unchecked these changes could lead to cervical cancer.

It is important to have your cervix examined by your GP or at a colposcopy clinic.

Next Steps

Your GP is the best person to advise you on the specific steps you need, this may include a referral for a smear or colposcopy.

We recommend that you book an appointment with your GP or healthcare provider within the next 2 weeks.

Important: Any changes to your  cervix should not get worse while you're waiting for your appointment.

A colposcopy is...

A simple procedure to look at your cervix.

Similar to having a smear at the GP, but it’s done in hospital, with specialist nurses and doctors.

Remember

Having a positive HPV result does not mean your partner has had sex with someone else while you have been together.

You might have HPV even if you have not been sexually active or not had a new partner for many years.
